# Stage 01 — Discovery & Strategy

## Purpose
Define what the website needs to achieve.

A typical website project moves through several stages, from strategy to launch. Different designers and agencies use slightly different names, but the process usually looks like this:

### Activities
- Business goals
- Target audience
- Competitor research
- Content planning
- Site structure (sitemap)
- SEO considerations

### Deliverables
- Project brief
- Sitemap
- User journeys
- Content outline

This stage absorbs the previous intake work. Use the questionnaire to gather initial information.

## Inputs
- Client brief or initial conversation
- Completed questionnaire (if using intake form)

## Outputs
- `output/project-brief.md` or `intake.md` — the client's completed answers and strategy notes
- Sitemap
- User journeys
- Content outline

## Process
1. Copy the questions from `questionnaire.md` into an email and send to the client (or use `client-intake-form.html`).
2. When the client replies, paste their answers into `output/intake.md` under each question.
3. Review answers — follow up by email if anything is missing or unclear.
4. Define business goals, target audience, competitor research.
5. Plan content and site structure (sitemap).
6. Consider SEO.
7. Produce deliverables: Project brief, Sitemap, User journeys, Content outline.
8. Update `../../MEMORY.md` — mark Stage 01 complete and current stage → 02-wireframing.

## Notes
Many projects actually do content work alongside design rather than strictly sequentially.

## Checkpoint
All questions answered or explicitly marked N/A. Sitemap and strategy deliverables saved before closing.
