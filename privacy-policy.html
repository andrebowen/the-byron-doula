Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information

Last updated: May 2026

1. Who we are

The Byron Doula is operated by Nathalie Solis, a professional doula based in Byron Bay and the Northern Rivers, NSW, Australia. Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, and how we handle it — in plain language.

2. Information we collect

We only collect information you give us directly:

  • Contact form — your name, email address, phone number, and message
  • Booking enquiries — your due date and any details you choose to share about your pregnancy or birth preferences
  • Email correspondence — messages you send us directly

3. How we use your information

Your information is used solely to:

  • Respond to your enquiry or booking request
  • Provide and coordinate doula services
  • Send appointment reminders or follow-up care information

We do not use your information for marketing without your explicit consent, and we never sell your data.

4. Sensitive information

Information about your pregnancy, birth, and health is sensitive. We treat it with the utmost care and confidentiality. It is shared only with you and, where necessary, with the healthcare providers directly involved in your care — and only with your knowledge.

5. Third-party services

This website uses the following third-party services, each with their own privacy policies:

  • Google Fonts — serves typefaces; may log your IP address
  • Calendly — used for booking consultations; governed by Calendly's privacy policy
  • Stripe — used for payment processing; we do not store your payment details

6. Cookies

This website does not use tracking or advertising cookies.

7. Data storage and security

Your information is stored securely and accessed only by Nathalie Solis. We take reasonable steps to protect it from unauthorised access, disclosure, or loss. We retain your information only for as long as necessary to provide services or meet legal obligations.

8. Your rights

Under the Australian Privacy Act 1988, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information
  • Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)

9. Contact

For any privacy questions or requests, please get in touch:
