Birth Preparation

Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does a doula do? ▼
A doula is a trained birth professional who provides continuous physical, emotional, and informational support during labor and birth. Unlike medical professionals, doulas don't perform medical procedures—we focus on supporting you and your partner. Research shows that doula support can improve birth outcomes and increase birth satisfaction.
Will you come to the hospital? ▼
Yes, I work with families planning hospital births, home births, and births at birthing centers. I'm comfortable working with all care providers and birth settings. During our initial consultation, we'll discuss your specific birth location and preferences.
How much does doula care cost? ▼
Both services are $150/hr. Parent coaching is available virtually. Birth preparation pricing depends on your needs and the number of sessions — get in touch and we'll put together something that works for you. Many families find that personalised support is one of the most valuable investments they make during this time.
Do you provide medical care? ▼
No, doulas are not medical professionals and do not provide medical care, diagnoses, or medical procedures. We work alongside your healthcare provider to support you. If you need medical advice, please consult with your doctor or midwife.
When should I hire a doula? ▼
Ideally, you'll connect with a doula in your second or third trimester, though I'm happy to work with you at any point in your pregnancy. Even late-pregnancy hiring allows time for meaningful preparation. If you're pregnant and interested, I recommend reaching out sooner rather than later to ensure availability.
What if I'm planning a C-section? ▼
I support families with all birth outcomes, including planned and emergency cesareans. Doula support during a cesarean includes prenatal preparation, emotional support, and postpartum recovery assistance. I work with hospitals and surgical teams to provide support while respecting their protocols.
