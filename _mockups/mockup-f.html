Scenario F — Visual Radio Cards

How it works: Same flow as E but the service selector uses image cards instead of list chips — 2×2 grid with photos, making the choice feel more tactile and visual. The ✓ badge snaps onto the selected card. The last card ("Not sure yet") spans both columns.

  • Pros: Photos make the services feel real and personal. More visually engaging than a chip list. Works especially well if each service has a distinct image.
  • Cons: Takes up more vertical space than chips. Images must be good — placeholder boxes look bad.
  • Best for: Visitors who respond to visual cues over text-only options.
