The 5-Step Process

How The Byron Doula website is built — and why each decision was made. Based on the 2026 web design framework for sites that convert visitors into clients.

1

Strategy First

Foundation

Define who the site is for, what visitors already know, and what you want them to do. Everything else flows from a single primary goal.

The one thing a visitor should do: contact Nathalie for a free initial consultation.
The Principle
Who is the site for?
Expectant mothers and their partners already considering doula support. They can be located in Byron Bay and surrounding areas, or globally via virtual sessions.
What do they already know?
They arrive knowing what a doula is — copy skips the basics and speaks to the emotional outcome. Show strong credentials to build trust and remove any doubts.
What is the single primary goal?
Contact Nathalie for a free initial consultation. Every family is different — Nathalie tailors a plan after the first conversation.
Everything else flows from this.
How We've Implemented It
  • Single CTA throughout: "Get in touch" — drives to a contact form, no payment friction
  • Services section uses information cards (no pricing) to inform, not transact
  • Free initial consultation badge and trust signals lower the barrier to contact
  • Every page either answers a trust question or routes to the contact section
2

Structure, Sitemap & Wireframes

Architecture

A clear sitemap and wireframes establish hierarchy and user flow before any visual design — preventing tangled sites and guiding visitors toward the goal.

The Principle
  • Map all pages and how they connect
  • Build wireframes before visual design
  • Establish hierarchy and user flow
  • Every path leads to the goal
How We've Implemented It
  • Three core pages: Home/Services, About, Reviews
  • Every page ends at the same destination: the contact/booking section
  • Navigation is minimal and familiar — no unexpected labels
  • Legal pages (privacy, terms, cookies) exist but are not surfaced in the primary nav
Sitemap defined Wireframes built User flow validated
3

Design That Converts

Visual Design

Clarity over flashy effects. Every design decision — font, colour, space, CTA — serves the visitor and the business goal.

The Principle
  • Typography that improves legibility and signals quality
  • A tight palette of 2–3 colours, each with a job
  • White space — let content breathe
  • One primary CTA, repeated consistently
How We've Implemented It
  • Cormorant Garamond (serif) for headings — warmth and premium feel; Inter (sans) for body — legible at any size
  • Primary sienna (#88511D), secondary muted sienna, tertiary golden yellow — three roles, no filler colours
  • MD3 spacing scale enforced globally — generous padding, no crowded sections
  • "Book a session" is the only primary button; secondary links support but don't compete
Type system Colour palette MD3 spacing Single CTA
4

Build for Usability & Maintainability

Build

Built on a platform the owner can actually manage. Logical, editable structures — not clever code that breaks the moment someone touches it.

The Principle
  • Platform the owner can manage themselves
  • Logical, editable structures
  • Avoid overly complex "clever" code
  • Proper training and ongoing support
How We've Implemented It
  • Pure HTML/CSS/JS — no CMS dependency, no build tools, opens in any browser
  • MD3 design tokens mean global changes (colour, type, shape) happen in one place
  • Consistent naming conventions and file structure across all pages
  • Copy lives directly in HTML — no database, no login required to edit text
No-framework build Token-driven design Owner training
5

Launch, SEO & Optimisation

Launch

Launch is the beginning, not the end. SEO built in from the start. Analytics tracking behaviour. Multiple contact options to reduce drop-offs.

The Principle
  • Treat launch as the beginning
  • SEO built in from the start (structure, copy, sitemap)
  • Analytics to track real behaviour
  • Multiple contact options to reduce drop-offs
How We've Implemented It
  • Semantic HTML throughout — nav, main, section, article, footer — correct document structure for crawlers
  • Meta titles and descriptions on every page; Open Graph tags for social sharing
  • Copy written for the search terms Nathalie's clients actually use
  • Contact form + direct email as fallback; analytics and sitemap.xml to be added at launch
Semantic HTML Meta & OG tags sitemap.xml Analytics Deploy

Key Takeaway

Design should serve the visitor and the business goal first. A great site guides people clearly, stays easy to update, and continues improving over time through data and optimisation.
