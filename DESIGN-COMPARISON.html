01 — Foundation

Design System

The single biggest change is the underlying design framework. v1 was built on Material Design 3 — a system designed for apps. The rebuild uses Earth & Grace — an editorial system purpose-built for this brand.

Earth & Grace was created specifically from this brand's colour and content brief — the result is a system that feels like it belongs.
02 — Navigation

Navigation

Aspectv1NewWhy
Background Transparent over hero → solid cream on scroll Always dark warm brown #2d1f16 Bug Fix The transparent state caused white logo/links on a light cream hero — completely invisible. The always-dark nav is always readable on every page, regardless of the section behind it.
Logo colour White when over hero, dark when scrolled Always cream #fbf9f5 on dark nav Bug Fix Single consistent treatment. No JavaScript colour toggle needed.
Visual weight Disappears into page at top Strong masthead — anchors the brand Improved The dark bar creates a clear frame. It reads like a premium editorial publication — every page feels like it belongs to the same brand.
CTA button Primary-colour pill (blends with page) Terracotta pill on dark nav — high contrast Improved Terracotta on near-black has ~5:1 contrast. The CTA pops immediately in the nav without competing with page content.
Mobile drawer Drawer present Full-screen overlay, large serif link text Improved Large, widely-spaced links are easy to tap. The serif style maintains the editorial tone inside the menu.
03 — Hero Section

Hero Section

The hero is the first thing a visitor sees. The most important trust signal for a doula is the face of the person. v1 buried Nathalie's photo lower on the page.

Aspectv1NewWhy
Layout Text centred over gradient background — no photo Split: text left + Nathalie portrait right New The first thing a prospective client needs to feel is connection to the person they're hiring. Showing Nathalie's face immediately — before any text is read — creates that connection faster.
Mobile layout Centred text, no photo above fold Portrait stacks above text — face first Improved On mobile, portrait-first means the emotional connection happens before reading. Text below the photo has more context and warmth.
Height 400–500px fixed 100svh — true full viewport Improved svh (small viewport height) accounts for mobile browser chrome correctly — 100svh actually fills the visible screen rather than overflowing it.
CTA buttons Primary + Secondary, horizontal Primary "Work With Me" + Ghost "Learn More" — pill shaped, flex-wrap Improved Pill buttons have higher visual weight and feel more intentional. flex-wrap ensures they stack naturally on narrow screens without breaking layout.
04 — Typography

Typography

Rolev1NewWhy
Display / Headlines Cormorant Garamond Fraunces Improved Fraunces is an optical-size variable serif with a warm, organic quality that suits the Byron Bay brief. It holds weight across screen sizes where Cormorant can appear hairline-thin on Windows and Android. The italic cuts add editorial expressiveness for pull quotes and hero text.
Body / UI DM Sans DM Sans (unchanged) Already the right call. High legibility, modern, pairs well with the serif headlines.
Scale Fixed px values per breakpoint clamp() fluid scaling Improved clamp(32px, 8vw, 52px) produces smooth type scaling from 320px to 1440px without breakpoint jumps or overflow.
Display size mobile 42px (separate mobile rule) Handled by clamp — auto-calculated Fewer rules to maintain. No chance of a breakpoint being missed.
05 — Colour System

Colour System

Known v1 bug: The surface and background tokens were mismatched — surface was old cream #FFF8F4 while background had been updated to a different container value. This caused sections to visually clash throughout the page.
Aspectv1NewWhy
Surface token Mismatched: surface ≠ background Bug #fbf9f5 used everywhere consistently Fixed A single warm cream surface value means every section reads as part of the same canvas. The v1 mismatch created subtle but jarring section-to-section clashes.
Primary hue rgb(136 81 29) — sienna/terracotta #8c4a2f — near-identical terracotta Brand colour continuity. The hue is preserved — just expressed more cleanly as a hex value.
Theme variants 6 theme files (light, dark, hc, mc × 2) 1 theme (light only) Simplified None of the 6 themes were exposed to users. Maintaining 6 colour variants added complexity with zero user-facing benefit for this project scope.
Dark nav colour Not present #2d1f16 — deep warm brown New Derived from the terracotta family at near-black luminosity. Warm undertone matches the brand palette — it doesn't read as cold corporate black.
Sand / secondary container rgb(255 220 194) — pinkish #efdec0 — warm sand Improved The new sand is warmer and more neutral — works better as a card/chip background without pulling pink on screens.
06 — Trust Strip

Trust Strip ("As Featured In")

Aspectv1NewWhy
Content Plain text names of publications Real publication logo images Improved Logos are instantly recognisable social proof. Text names require reading and feel unverified — they could be made up. A logo carries the visual authority of the publication.
Treatment Styled text Greyscale + 55% opacity Improved Industry-standard "press strip" treatment. Muted logos don't compete with the brand palette and feel appropriately understated.
Background Page background surface-container-low with top/bottom borders Light tonal separation marks the strip as a distinct content band without a harsh background change.
07 — Services

Services & Pricing

Aspectv1NewWhy
Structure Cards in a section on the home page only Dedicated services.html New Visitors who want pricing details can't get them from a 3-card home page section. A dedicated page gives room for includes lists, photos, pricing, and context — everything needed to convert an enquiry.
Page header Not applicable Dark text banner — no hero image Improved A services page doesn't need a large decorative photo at the top — the photos belong to the service panels themselves. The clean dark header states the page purpose immediately and flows straight into content.
Layout 3-column card grid Editorial split panels — photo bleeds full height to viewport edge, copy in adjacent column Improved Photos are given real presence rather than being thumbnail-sized inside a card. The alternating left/right pattern creates visual rhythm. No card wrapper means no competing decorative elements — just image and words.
Pricing Not shown $150/hr — plain body copy in the text column New Transparent pricing builds trust and pre-qualifies enquiries. Presented as quiet body text rather than a highlighted price tag — consistent with the understated brand tone.
Includes lists None 6-item checklist per service New Clients often don't know what a doula does. Explicit includes lists answer "what am I paying for?" before the question is asked.
Service numbering Not applicable None — service name headings stand alone With only two services on the page, sequential numbers (01, 02) add decoration without meaning. The headings are sufficient.
08 — Testimonials

Testimonials & Reviews

Aspectv1NewWhy
Content Placeholder text quotes Real quotes from testimonials.json Fixed Authenticity is the entire point of testimonials. Placeholder text actively undermines trust.
Avatars No photos Real client portrait photos New A face next to a quote makes it feel like a real person said it. Anonymous text blocks feel like they could have been fabricated.
Volume 3 cards on home page 3 on home page + dedicated reviews.html with 9 cards, featured quote, stats New Depth of social proof matters. A wall of 9 glowing, photo-backed reviews from named clients is far more persuasive than 3 cards. The featured quote and stats (★ 5.0, 7+ years, 100% recommend) set expectations before reading.
Mobile scrolling Grid (can feel cramped on small screens) Horizontal scroll on mobile, grid on desktop Improved Horizontal swipe is a natural gesture on mobile. It implies "there's more" and invites exploration without needing to show all cards at once.
09 — About

About Section

Aspectv1NewWhy
Structure One section on the home page Dedicated about.html New For a service business where the person is the product, the about page is a primary conversion driver. It deserves its own URL, its own SEO value, and full creative space.
Photo layout Image right, text left (desktop) Sticky portrait panel — stays visible while scrolling story Improved As a visitor reads Nathalie's story, her face remains in view. This is a pattern from long-form editorial journalism — it keeps the human connection active throughout reading.
Philosophy Not present 3 philosophy cards: Evidence-Based, Non-Judgmental, Continuously Present New These three statements directly address the top anxieties of first-time doula clients: "Will she judge my choices?", "Will she actually stay?", "Can I trust her information?" Each card is an answer.
Credentials Not shown 4 credential cards: certification, years, membership, first aid New Expectant parents are trusting this person with one of the most significant moments of their lives. Specifics matter — "Doula Australia certified" and "first aid renewed annually" are trust signals that generic wellness copy cannot replace.
10 — Contact

Contact

Aspectv1NewWhy
Structure Single-column form in a home page section Dedicated contact.html, two-column layout New A dedicated page gives the contact form proper real estate and its own URL for direct linking (from Instagram bio, for example).
Info panel None Photo + "What happens next" + location + Instagram link New "What happens next?" (1→2→3 steps) reduces the anxiety of submitting. Many visitors abandon contact forms because they don't know what they're committing to. Making the next steps explicit drives completions.
Form fields Name, email, service, message + Phone, due date Improved Phone lets Nathalie call instead of email-tag. Due date tells her whether the family is still bookable and lets her personalise her first response.
Post-submit Default FormSubmit page Custom thankyou.html Improved The thank-you page is an opportunity to reassure the visitor ("I'll be in touch within 48 hours"), maintain brand design, and give them a path back to the site.
11 — Device Experience

Why It's Better on Mobile

The new site is genuinely mobile-first — designed for 390px and scaled up, not the other way around.

Mobile Improvements

  • Portrait photo stacks above hero text — Nathalie's face is the first thing seen, before reading a word
  • Fluid type with clamp() — no text overflow or awkward size jumps between breakpoints on any phone size
  • Pill buttons have generous 44px+ touch targets with padding — compliant with WCAG 2.5.5
  • Testimonials use horizontal scroll — natural swipe gesture, implies "there's more" without cramming a grid onto a 390px screen
  • FAQ accordion collapses by default — saves significant scroll distance; a long FAQ page on mobile is unusable
  • Footer stacks to single column — v1's 3-column grid on mobile would have squeezed text into 3 narrow columns
  • Full-screen nav drawer with large serif links — easy to tap, easy to read, easy to close
  • 100svh hero — uses small viewport height unit which accounts for mobile browser chrome correctly, unlike 100vh which overflows
  • Service section stacks copy above detail panel — content hierarchy is preserved in the correct reading order on mobile
  • About portrait displays above story text on mobile — sticky scroll is desktop-only, but the image is still present at the top on mobile
12 — Device Experience

Why It's Better on Desktop

Desktop Improvements

  • Split hero uses the full viewport width purposefully — the left/right balance creates an editorial composition that centred-text-on-gradient cannot
  • Sticky photo panel on about.html — as a visitor reads Nathalie's story on a large screen, her portrait stays in view; emotional connection is maintained throughout
  • Services alternating layout — each service block gets full horizontal breathing room with photo and pricing panel side by side; creates natural visual rhythm across the page
  • Reviews 3-column grid fills the page properly — 9 real reviews with photos look intentional and substantial on a wide screen
  • Contact two-column layout — info panel and form at equal width feels considered and balanced; not just a form dropped on a white page
  • Trust strip logos scale correctly to desktop without gaps or over-sized logos — height-constrained with auto width
  • Max content width of 1200px — prevents line lengths from becoming unreadable on ultra-wide screens
  • About photo has correct 4/5 aspect ratio and clips intelligently — always shows Nathalie's face regardless of viewport height
  • Dark nav creates a strong frame at the top of the page — on large screens, a disappearing nav makes the page feel unanchored
